Created as a part of ASTRAL.INSTITUTE 2025 at Swimbourne.

The starmap project collected the data of 60k+ stars from the GAIA and SIMBAD astronomy databases.

Stars were filtered, visible stars (stars with apparent magnitudes of less than 8), were compiled.
This catalouge was then amended with more than 100 very bright stars from outside the extracted GAIA catalouge.
(the GAIA satelite is very sensitive, it does not observe known bright stars to preserve onboard instruments)

Using the Python PIL module and a premade star template, these stars were drawn on a large png canvas.
For each star taking the template, resizing and tinting. Each star comprised of a inner white template and an outer tinted template colored with black body color corrosponding to the star's tempature.

Constellation data came from the IAU (International Astronomical Union).
This currently uses conventional Western skylore, with support for the constalations of Australian Indigenous skylore of the Boorong clan.
A copy of the JSON file for western constalations may be found HERE.
Constallation data for Boorong skylore may be found within files of Stellarium.

The specific renderer has modifyable configuration that may be adjusted.
Star template image itself may also be swapped out.


Note: skymap is aligned with poles of the earth (uses RA DEC), it is not aligned with the ecliptic plane
